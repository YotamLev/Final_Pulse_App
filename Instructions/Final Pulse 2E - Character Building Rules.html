Overview & Creation Order

A character is built in four stages:

  1. Traits — choose Mortal and Vampire Traits (optional).
  2. Skills — spend 15 XP across the Skill Trees.
  3. Disciplines — unlock up to 3 Disciplines, then spend 10 XP raising their levels.
  4. Clan — optional; join the one Clan (if any) whose requirements your Traits and Disciplines satisfy.

Experience Points

Skill XP and Discipline XP are separate pools and cannot be mixed:

  • Creation Skill XP: 15, spent only on Skills.
  • Creation Discipline XP: 10, spent only on Disciplines.

After creation, the Storyteller awards Earned XP, which may be spent on either pool once its creation allotment is exhausted.

Skill cost

Each dot you personally invest in a skill costs XP equal to the effective level it brings the skill to — that is, its static base (dots inherited from a parent skill at the branch point, see below) plus the dot number you're buying. So the 1st own dot costs base + 1, the 2nd costs base + 2, and so on. A skill with no parent (base 0) costs 1 XP for its 1st dot, 2 for its 2nd, etc. — the classic 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 XP curve. Custom skills always have base 0.

Discipline cost

Raising a Discipline from level L to L+1 costs L+1 XP. So level 1 costs 1 XP, level 2 costs 2 more (3 total), up to level 5 costing 5 more (15 total).

Traits

Every Trait has a cost (some, like curses, have negative cost). Total cost of all Mortal + Vampire Traits combined cannot exceed +2. You may take up to 8 Traits total; 6 or fewer is recommended.

Skill Trees

Skills are grouped into 5 trees. When a skill branches from a parent skill at a given dot threshold, the parent's dots up to that threshold count as a static base added on top of the dots you invest directly in the child — so a skill's effective (practical) level is parent's static base + your own dots. This chains: a skill three branches deep inherits the accumulated base of everything above it.

Below, indentation shows the branching hierarchy — a nested skill branches from the skill directly above it at the dot count noted.

Scheming

  • Basic Analytical up to 2 dots
    Inferring secrets, planning ambushes, investigating crime scenes.
    • Finance up to 5 dots branches from Basic Analytical 2 dots
      Manage money, embezzle, launder, follow money trails.
    • Logistics up to 5 dots branches from Basic Analytical 2 dots
      Supplying, smuggling, organizing.
    • Investigation up to 5 dots branches from Basic Analytical 2 dots
      Interrogate, pick up clues, scan vast text sources.
    • Social Academics up to 5 dots branches from Basic Analytical 2 dots
      History, Archeology, Sociology, Anthropology, Philosophy, Literature.
    • Mortal Politics up to 5 dots branches from Basic Analytical 2 dots
      Organize large institutions, find who holds sway in mortal society, understand mortal laws.
      • Undead Politics up to 2 dots branches from Mortal Politics 3 dots
        Find out who holds sway and makes decisions in vampiric society.

Manipulation

  • Basic Manipulation up to 2 dots
    Push people's buttons, convince, convert.
    • Court Etiquette up to 5 dots branches from Basic Manipulation 2 dots
      Rules of Vampiric society, traditions, proper forms of address.
      • Boon Economy up to 2 dots branches from Court Etiquette 3 dots
        Understand how Boons and Debts amongst Vampires work and use them to your advantage.
    • Social Chameleon up to 5 dots branches from Basic Manipulation 2 dots
      Integrate in mortal society and circles, understand and perform mortal culture.
      • Mortal Society Status up to 2 dots branches from Social Chameleon 1 dots
        Learn to rise in Status and recognition, and wield them over mortals.
    • Deception up to 5 dots branches from Basic Manipulation 2 dots
      Learn to lie and deceive.
    • Persuasion up to 5 dots branches from Basic Manipulation 2 dots
      Learn to convince and corrupt.
    • Intimidation up to 5 dots branches from Basic Manipulation 2 dots
      Learn to frighten and strong-arm your lessers.
  • Insight up to 5 dots
    See through lies, uncover motives and emotions.

Thrashing

  • Brawl up to 2 dots
    Up-close and personal fighting. Battle-awareness, willingness to suffer and impose pain.
    • Martial Arts up to 5 dots branches from Brawl 2 dots
      Fighting face-to-face using fists, kicks, chokeholds etc.
    • Swordsmanship up to 5 dots branches from Brawl 2 dots
      Fighting using bladed weapons: swords, scimitars, rapiers, katanas.
    • Bludgeoning up to 5 dots branches from Brawl 2 dots
      Adept at blunt-force trauma: maces, baseball bats, rocks.
  • Guns up to 5 dots
    Can shoot, clean, reload guns. Knows how to handle them.
    • Marksmanship up to 2 dots branches from Guns 5 dots or Projectiles 5 dots
      Can shoot long range, picking up targets as a sniper.
  • Projectiles up to 5 dots
    Can shoot bows, throw knives, and use other long-range weapons.
  • Dodge up to 5 dots
    Dodge physical attacks. Roll if actively dodging, or take half rounded-down as difficulty to hit you.
  • Athletics up to 5 dots
    Can jump, climb, crawl, and stay upright.

Resourcefulness

  • Awareness up to 5 dots
    Notice threats, recognize surroundings.
    • Streetwise up to 5 dots branches from Awareness 2 dots
      Can get drugs, understand gang territory, notice weird looks, survive in urban areas.
    • Survival up to 5 dots branches from Awareness 2 dots
      Understand terrain, navigation, find sanctuary from the sun, survive in non-urban areas.
  • Stealth up to 5 dots
    Can hide, sneak, skulk, notice gazes.
    • Larceny up to 5 dots branches from Stealth 2 dots
      Can picklock, pickpocket, carjack, break & enter.
  • Technology up to 5 dots
    Understand common technology as something to use and manipulate.
    • Hacking up to 5 dots branches from Technology 2 dots
      Understand operating systems, cryptography, and communication well enough to abuse, infiltrate, and distort.
  • Medicine up to 7 dots

    • Medical Forensics up to 5 dots branches from Medicine 2 dots
      Understand evidence collection methods, DNA, how to make deaths look natural.
    • Poisons up to 5 dots branches from Medicine 2 dots
      Understand dosages, poisons, drug overdoses.

Experimentation

  • Curiosity up to 2 dots
    Morbid interest in pushing the limits of what current society can do and is willing to do.
    • Science up to 5 dots branches from Curiosity 2 dots
      Understand the scientific method and current mainstream science.
      • Biology up to 5 dots branches from Science 2 dots
        Understand biological processes in mortals, animals, bacteria, viruses.
        • Weird Science up to 5 dots branches from Biology 2 dots
          Willing to experiment on unwilling mortals; can create mutants, targeted viruses. Uses are specific, unstable, and often unreproducible.

    • Occult up to 5 dots branches from Curiosity 2 dots
      Research forbidden lore, secret weaknesses, and rituals.
      • Demonology up to 5 dots branches from Occult 3 dots
        Research demons and means of summoning them.

Custom Skills

Players may invent their own skill (e.g. Dance, Chess, Agriculture) with a Storyteller-set max dots. Custom skills have no parent/base — cost follows the same 1, 2, 3… curve from 0.

Discipline Trees

A vampire may unlock up to 3 Disciplines. Each Discipline has a level from 0–5. Raising a Discipline's level lets you acquire that many powers total from it (one power slot per level), chosen from any power at or below your current level whose prerequisite (if any) you already hold.

Auspex

Enhance senses, peer into other dimensions, telepathy.

  • Level 1
    • Enhance Senses
      Add Auspex level to sensory input: seeing, hearing, etc.
    • Sense The Unseen
      Roll Auspex to find hidden and invisible creatures or objects.
    • Impulse
      Ask 1 question per scene about: danger, emotions, or specific secrets.
  • Level 2
    • Read Aura
      Pay 1 Blood, roll Auspex — margin is number of questions you ask about a target creature, or skim surface thoughts of people nearby.
    • Spirit's Touch
      Pay 1 Blood, roll Auspex — margin is number of questions you ask about a target item or building.
    • Anticipation
      Pay 1 Blood, add Auspex to rolls made to dodge or defend against all kinds of attacks and dangers, for a scene.
  • Level 3
    • Telepathy
      Pay 1 Blood, talk to 1 target you know for a scene. If target is unwilling or far, roll Auspex. Lasts for a scene.
    • Write Script
      Pay 1 Blood, choose a short phrase and a target whom you are in conversation with - the target will utter it, in some form.
    • Clairvoyance
      Pay 1 Blood, roll Auspex — margin is number of questions about events during the last day in a large area.
  • Level 4
    • Tempt Fate
      Pay 3 Blood, cause another to reroll a roll. Once per night.
    • Scry requires Telepathy
      When using Telepathy you can roll Auspex to avoid the target sensing you. When Telepathy is active, experience the target's senses in real time.

Animalism

Control animals, act on your inner animal.

  • Level 1
    • Ghoul Animal
      Pay 1 Blood to ghoul an animal to serve you. Up to Animalism level simultaneously. Wants to serve like a loyal dog but no greater intelligence.
    • Mark Territory
      Pay 1 Blood in a place you own to impose difficulty on rivals in it.
    • Confront the Beast
      Pay 1 Blood, roll Animalism — relax or enrage another Vampire's Beast.
  • Level 2
    • Borrowed Wisdom
      Drinking from an animal gives +3 to +5 in things it excels at until next feed. In addition, animal blood becomes as nourishing as human Blood - a large dog can give 3 Blood, a wolf can give 4 Blood, a horse 6.
    • Abomination requires Ghoul Animal
      Pay 1 Blood to make monstrous changes to a ghouled animal (enlarge, add HP, add features). Max changes = Animalism level.
    • Unliving Hive
      A swarm of small animals lives in your dead flesh. Add Animalism level to Intimidation rolls, half Animalism (rounded down) to close-range attacks.
  • Level 3
    • The Wild Hunt
      Pay 1 Blood, enrage all animals to seek and attack a target — your Animalism is their roll.
    • Wild Whispers
      Pay 1 Blood, communicate with any animal fully for a scene.
    • Summon Animal
      Summon a specific type of animal or specify characteristics. Roll Animalism if very specific or uncommon in area.
  • Level 5
    • Wild Escape requires Ghoul Animal
      When reduced to 0 HP, instantly move your consciousness to a nearby ghouled animal instead of torpor. Getting a drop of your original blood lets you reform your old body.

Celerity

Superhuman speed and agility.

  • Level 1
    • Speed
      Add Celerity level to rolls involving speed: running, slashing, dodging. Add half Celerity (rounded down) to rolls of precision.
  • Level 2
    • Blink
      Pay 1 Blood, move super-fast in your turn: walk on water, on vertical walls, between the blades of a spinning jet engine.
  • Level 3
    • Bullet Speed requires Speed
      When using Speed, add Celerity level (instead of half) to rolls of precision, including shooting.
  • Level 4
    • Hover
      The vampire can hover slightly above ground.
    • Fast Strike
      Pay 2 Blood, you can take 2 actions in a turn without penalties.
  • Level 5
    • Unholy Flight requires Hover
      Pay 1 Blood, the vampire can fly at running speed for a scene.
    • Uncanny Dodge requires Hover
      When attacked by something you can dodge, pay 1 Blood, lower incoming HP damage by 2, as a reflex.

Dominate

Puppeteer others, wipe memories.

  • Level 1
    • Draw On Instinct
      Cause a mortal to obey a simple command that is some form of: Flee ('run away!'), Freeze ('kneel before me,'), or Fight ('shoot that guy!').
    • Cloud Mind
      Cause a mortal to forget the last 5 minutes and the next 2 minutes.
  • Level 2
    • Mesmerize
      Pay 1 Blood, target a mortal (roll Dominate if they resist), cause a simple task for about a scene. Task can't be suicidal. Mortals remember the task but not why.
    • Modify Memory
      Pay 1 Blood, target a mortal, roll Dominate, alter memories. Cannot extract information.
  • Level 3
    • Implant Trigger
      Pay 1 Blood, allows Mesmerize, Cloud Mind, and Modify Memory to have a trigger. Lasts for Dominate level days or until triggered.
    • Terminal Decree requires Mesmerize
      Allows suicidal tasks with Mesmerize.
  • Level 4
    • Dominate Your Kind
      Allows using Dominate powers on vampires. Very hard and very risky.
    • Mass Dominate
      Target more than 1 mortal with Dominate, paying 1 Blood per extra mortal. Max targets = Dominate level.
    • Rationalize
      Targets of Dominate powers will attempt to reconcile their actions & memories, possibly scarring their personality.

Fortitude

Heal, absorb damage, enhance your capacity.

  • Level 1
    • Rapid Healing
      Each 1 Blood paid allows healing 2 HP instead of 1.
    • Toughness
      Add 2 max HP per level of Fortitude.
  • Level 2
    • Prowess From Pain
      Each HP damage you have, up to Fortitude level, can be added to physical attacks or stressed efforts.
    • Draught of Endurance
      Your ghouls add your Fortitude level to their HP. A vampire drinking at least 1 of your Blood gets your Fortitude powers for the night.
  • Level 3
    • Flesh of Marble
      Pay 2 Blood, take no damage for 1 turn.
    • Mind Fortress
      Add Fortitude level to rolls to resist mental powers.
  • Level 4
    • Last Stand requires Flesh of Marble
      If reduced to 0 HP, Flesh of Marble activates automatically to prevent Torpor, as long as Blood is -8 or higher. Blood cost paid as normal.
  • Level 5
    • Endurance requires Flesh of Marble
      Flesh of Marble costs 1 Blood instead of 2.

Nightmare

Scare, unsettle, put to sleep.

  • Level 1
    • Haunt Dream
      Appear in a mortal's dreams, give them messages. Can be activated during day and from torpor.
    • Lullaby
      Put an unsuspecting mortal to sleep, or a group of mortals by paying 1 Blood. Suspecting mortals or stressful environments may require a Nightmare roll.
    • It Was All a Bad Dream
      Cause all mortals in a scene to believe whatever scary thing they just saw was a dream or has a mundane explanation.
    • Melpominee
      Cast your voice to sound as if coming from a point you can see.
  • Level 2
    • Dread Gaze
      Add Nightmare to Intimidation rolls, create scary illusions of your appearance. A +3 margin can paralyze a target for a turn.
    • Haunting Ground
      Activate to cause mortals to subconsciously avoid an area when you are there.
  • Level 3
    • Paranoia requires Haunt Dream
      Pay 1 Blood, unnerve a target you talk to, causing up to -4 for the night.
    • Illusion
      Pay 1 Blood, create a small motionless illusion affecting one sense. Each additional sense, motion, or size increase costs 1 more Blood.
  • Level 4
    • Summon Nightmare requires Paranoia
      Pay 1 Blood, attack a target with its worst fear, rolling Nightmare, inflicting Willpower damage.

Obfuscate

Hide, appear differently.

  • Level 1
    • Fade
      Low-level hypnotism makes all mortals avert their eyes and ignore you, seeing you as a forgettable nobody.
    • Cloak of Shadows
      Standing still or moving obscured against a backdrop allows you to be totally ignored by mortals — they see you as an object or vegetation.
  • Level 2
    • Vanish
      Pay 1 Blood, be completely invisible for a scene. Intentionally stopping from hiding, by attacking, talking loudly etc, - ends the invisibility.
    • Disguise
      Pay 1 Blood, alter your appearance to appear as another person. Copying someone exactly is much harder.
    • Labyrinth
      Pay 1 Blood, cause mortals to get confused and lost around you, having difficulty navigating. Lasts a scene or pay 1 extra Blood for the whole day.
    • Silent Hunter
      When using Fade, Cloak of Shadows, or Vanish — the vampire can also completely silence sounds from themselves.
  • Level 3
    • Ambusher
      Pay 1 Blood, make Obfuscate powers work on other Vampires for a scene. Those with Auspex can roll against your Obfuscate.
    • Cloak the Gathering
      Extend Obfuscate powers to willing vampires who stay close. Pay 1 extra Blood per vampire per scene.

Potence

Strength, brutality.

  • Level 1
    • Vigor
      Add Potence to rolls involving strength and muscles: swinging a sword, jumping, bending metal.
  • Level 2
    • Shockwave
      Pay 1 Blood, your attacks count against all in close-range to you instead of choosing 1 enemy.
    • Bloody Strangle
      Pay 1 Blood while grappling a target — that target cannot use supernatural powers, except Potence and Fortitude, as long as you hold it.
    • Brutal Feed
      Can drain an entire mortal in 1 turn.

Presence

Enrapture, charm, control masses.

  • Level 1
    • Awe
      Add Presence to Social rolls.
    • Summon
      Call a mortal you know to you. They will do all in their power to reach you.
  • Level 2
    • Midnight Command
      Pay 1 Blood, add your Presence to rolls made by your ghouls and mortals you lead for a scene.
    • Hypnotize
      Pay 1 Blood, roll Presence, paralyze a group of mortals or a vampire for as long as you keep using turns on this power.
    • Affect Mood
      Choose an emotion and project it to all mortals in the scene.
  • Level 3
    • Sway
      Pay 2 Blood, cause a great crowd of mortals to absolutely believe in a plausible cause. They will endanger themselves for it. Lasts a scene.
  • Level 4
    • Art Necro
      Create a piece of art that can project one of your Presence powers on viewers.

Protean

Shapeshift.

  • Level 1
    • Animorph
      Pay 1 Blood, assume the shape of an animal (mouse to tiger) for a scene. Transforming takes a turn.
    • Feral Weapons
      Extend fangs and claws, add your Protean to close-range physical attacks.
    • Bone Armor
      Pay 1 or more Blood, gain 2 extra HP per Blood for a scene.
    • Bone Bullets
      Shoot teeth, bone spurs, or nails as natural weapons.
  • Level 2
    • Separate Limb
      Pay 1 Blood, separate a limb and grant it independence. It serves you but only has senses it physically has. Up to Protean level simultaneously.
  • Level 3
    • Mist Form
      Pay 1 Blood, spend two turns to transform into a cloud of mist — untouchable save by fire and sunlight. Cannot spend more than two scenes as mist.
    • Internal Shift
      Move your internal organs at will — shift your heart to dodge a stake, make it seem to pound, terrify onlookers.
  • Level 4
    • Fleshcraft
      pay 2 Blood, take two turns to transform your body: rotate your head 180°, move your hands to your belly, even attaching organs from other creatures, like having extra hands or spider eyes. Note that sensory organs offer no sensory input, but muscle, bone, chitin etc. do. wings require a giant bird, and flying with them is very hard. Up to Protean level changes simultaneously.
  • Level 5
    • Szlachta
      You can use your Protean powers on your ghouls by touching them. This is very painful. Pay Blood prices as usual.

Arrete

Supernatural intelligence.

  • Level 1
    • Calculating
      Add your Arrete to mental actions requiring intelligence, typically in the Scheming Skill Tree.
    • Perfect Recall
      Pay 1 Blood, retroactively remember a detail from something you have seen, heard, or read.
  • Level 2
    • Borrowed Knowledge
      When feeding from a victim, gain a bonus (1-3 dice) to areas of expertise they had until next feeding.
    • The Common Denominator
      Pay 1 Blood, learn any human language for a scene. Pay 2 Blood to decipher any encoded message you can access.
  • Level 3
    • Perfectionism
      You can use Willpower to reroll up to 5 dice, instead of 3.
    • Oral History requires Borrowed Knowledge
      When feeding from a victim, gain a temporary custom skill at level 4 in an area of expertise the victim had, for the night. Up to Arrete level simultaneous skills. This does not interrupt Borrowed Knowledge.

Necromancy

Decay, raise lesser undead.

  • Level 1
    • Touch of Oblivion
      Pay 1 Blood, cripple and age a body part with a single touch. Roll Necromancy and relevant skills to touch an unwilling creature.
    • Ashes to Ashes
      Turn a body to fine ash, eliminating all blood and gore. Can be used on rival zombies.
    • Kill the Vibe
      Roll Mortifex (Necromancy) to cancel Presence and Nightmare powers.
  • Level 2
    • Raise Corpse
      Pay 1 Blood to raise a zombie using your Necromancy to act. Lasts for a night. The zombie obeys your commands as long as it can percieve you. Up to Necromancy level simultaneously.
    • Summon Wraith
      Pay 1 Blood to summon a ghost. Convince them to help — they can spy and frighten but not move anything materially except weak wind and sound.
    • Ossuary Explosion
      Pay 1 Blood, cause a body to explode, dealing Necromancy level damage to those nearby. No roll required.
  • Level 3
    • Zombie Legion requires Raise Corpse
      Undead created with Raise Corpse last to the next night as long as they aren't in sunlight. In addition, these undead serve even if they can't percieve who raised them.

Druidic

Control plants.

  • Level 1
    • Frost Touch
      Chill objects, freeze areas of water, or inflict frostbite with a touch.
    • Disband Beasts
      Roll Druidic to counter Animalism powers.
    • Druidcraft
      Cause minor changes in vegetation: flowers wither or bloom, mushrooms sprout.
  • Level 2
    • Upend the Course
      Pay 1 Blood, somewhat affect the weather of a city area. Summon clouds, rain, or snow for the night.
    • Wrath of Zeus
      Pay 1 Blood, direct lightning to strike a target, rolling Druidic.
    • Earth Meld
      Pay 1 Blood, sink into and become one with soil. Leave when you wish. You can travel slowly through natural earth and wood in this state.
    • Root Grasp
      Pay 1 Blood, command roots, vines, branches, or other living plants to move, entangle, or immobilize targets. Roll Druidic. Lasts a scene.
    • Sweet Sap
      You can drink Blood from trees, killing them. A large old tree gives 2 Blood, and small bushes give nothing.
  • Level 3
    • Malakan's Gift
      Pay 1 Blood, animate a tree or major plant as a servant for a night. The older the plant is - the more powerful it is as a servant.
  • Level 4
    • Foul Wind
      Pay 2 Blood, strong winds can lift you to fly, and you can direct strong wind to push people and objects around.

Blood Sorcery

Bend blood.

  • Level 1
    • Bend Blood
      Control spilled blood, making it flow or float in the air. Up to 2 Liters at a time, and the max force it can apply is 3 kg.
    • A Taste for Blood
      By tasting blood, detect many facts: age, birthplace, Sire (if Vampire), Disciplines (if Vampire), emotions when blood was spilled. Additional facts may require a Blood Sorcery roll.
  • Level 2
    • Bend Veins requires Bend Blood
      by paying 3 Blood, you can use Bend Blood even on blood inside another's body, rolling Blood Sorcery, for a scene. Causing damage is very difficult; slowing movement or causing a vampire to vomit blood is easy.
  • Level 3
    • Telekinesis requires Bend Blood
      by paying 1 Blood, Your Bend Blood power can now control up to 10 Liters of blood, and apply some force (roughly ~50 kg) at a time, for a turn.
    • Scorpion's Touch
      Turn your own spilled vitae corrosive, melting whatever it touches. 1 Blood extracted is enough to melt a hole through a regular door.
    • Vanigma
      You can research and cast various spells and rituals. Describe your desited effect to the Storyteller, and they will present you with the Scheme difficulty to research & cast it.
  • Level 5
    • Cauldron of Blood
      Pay 2 Blood, boil an enemy's blood in their veins, causing Blood Sorcery level damage in a turn to a target you can reach. No roll required.

Shadow Sorcery

Grab others by their shadow, spy through shadows.

  • Level 1
    • Dim the Lights
      Cause lights to dim or flicker, subject to your control.
    • Shadow Perspective
      Look into a shadow and see from another shadow nearby.
  • Level 2
    • Shadow Step
      Pay 1 Blood, slip into a shadow and re-emerge into another shadow close by.
    • Grab the Effigy
      Pay 1 Blood, for a scene you can beat, grapple, or use another's shadow as if it were the target. Roll Shadow Sorcery, adding Brawl if relevant.
  • Level 3
    • Shadow Step Mastery requires Shadow Step
      Shadow Step costs 1 Blood and lasts for the scene.
    • Shoot the Shadow
      Pay 2 Blood, cause one attack that hit you to hit your shadow instead, reflexively (no action required).
  • Level 4
    • Shadow Avatar
      Pay 3 Blood, cause your shadow to materialize and act for a scene, acting as your double.

Clans

Clans are optional. Each has requirements built from your Traits and unlocked Disciplines — you may qualify for and join at most one Clan.

Ventrue

The self-styled rulers of vampire society, masters of control, who believe it is their birthright to lead.

Recruitment: Generals, Lords, Gang Leaders

Requirements: Trait: Selective Taste; Discipline: Dominate

Bonus: Connected — you gain significant advantages to find useful vampires and advance your status. In a social scene, for one target, if you ask for and recieve respect (in the form of proper addressing, a bow, etc.), you gain +2 on rolls to convince, intimidate, or strong-arm the target. If you do not recieve the respect you asked for, you gain +2 on all attack rolls gainst target.

Suggested Disciplines: Fortitude, Presence

Dracul

The main competitors of the Ventrue, fiercely stubborn. Known to ruin potential Embracees' lives to 'test' whether they are cut out for the Clan.

Recruitment: Crusaders, Fierce Nationalists, Merchant Princes

Requirements: Trait: Territorial or Archaic or Folkloric Bane

Bonus: Vengeful — you can Blood Surge and pay only half when pursuing vengeance against one target at a time, once per scene.

Suggested Disciplines: Dominate, Protean, Potence

Toreador

Connoisseurs of ascendant beauty and emotion, drawn irresistibly to the artistic and the sublime.

Recruitment: Artists, Diplomats, Charismatic Clergy

Requirements: Trait: Infatuation or Beautiful or Rare Specialist or World-Renowned Specialist; Discipline: Presence

Bonus: Gentle Touch — gain +2 on rolls to blood-bond others, and you can Blood Surge and pay only half when convincing or manipulating others.

Suggested Disciplines: Auspex, Celerity

Nosferatu

Hidden eyes and ears of the vampire world, cursed with a grotesque appearance that separates them from mortal society.

Recruitment: Spymasters, Assassins, Mercenaries

Requirements: Trait: Ugly or Corpse-Like; Discipline: Obfuscate

Bonus: Cryptomania — you heal Willpower when finding hidden information or things due to a focused, unnerving curiosity. Gain an advantage convincing Vampires to share information via trade.

Suggested Disciplines: Nightmare, Shadow Sorcery, Animalism

Brujah

Philosophers, fighters, rebels, gladiators. A history of great thinkers, generals, and revolutionaries.

Recruitment: Anarchists, Soldiers, Disloyal Knights

Requirements: Trait: Prone to Rage; Discipline: Potence or Celerity

Bonus: Freedom — heals Willpower twice as fast.

Suggested Disciplines: Presence, Potence, Celerity

Gangrel

Wild vampires described as closest to their Beast, who maintain they are simply best at interacting with it.

Recruitment: Survivalists, Conquistadors, Raiders

Requirements: Trait: Ravenous; Discipline: Animalism or Protean

Bonus: Can stay awake an hour after dawn and wake up half an hour before sunset.

Suggested Disciplines: Animalism, Protean, Fortitude

Malkavian

Afflicted by a supernatural madness — the clan of seers and oracles. The most heterogeneous clan.

Recruitment: Odd Scientists, Genius Detectives, Shamans

Requirements: At least one qualifying mental-illness Trait (Paranoid, Dissociative Episodes, Depressive Episodes, Manic Episodes, Psychotic Episodes, Panic Disorder, Post-Traumatic Stress, Delusional Belief, or Selective Mutism), from before or caused by the Embrace

Bonus: Prophetic — you can make a cryptic, vague, or riddle prophecy and have it be fulfilled. Only one prophecy active at a time.

Suggested Disciplines: Auspex, Nightmare, Arrete

Tremere

Mages who sought immortality. In their greed, they instigated a terrifying magical experiment, damning themselves to a hell of their own making.

Recruitment: Occultists, Pagans, Academics

Requirements: Trait: Rare Specialist or World-Renowned Specialist; Discipline: Blood Sorcery

Bonus: Ritual Magic — can find other Tremere to cast spells together; Somewhat tolerated by other mages.

Suggested Disciplines: Auspex, Arrete

Hecata

A strange clan steeped in death, practising the arts of necromancy. Favors Embracing from their own mortal family or those with useful outside connections.

Recruitment: Bankers, Arms Dealers, Monks

Requirements: Trait: Painful Bite or Selective Mutism; Discipline: Necromancy

Bonus: Private — spend 1 Willpower to automatically succeed against Insight, Aura Reading, or Dominate.

Suggested Disciplines: Fortitude, Potence
